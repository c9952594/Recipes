## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Prepare the onion, garlic, halloumi, lentils, tomatoes and ciabatta.
2. Put the ciabatta chunks and tomatoes on a baking tray. Toss with 15ml olive oil, salt and pepper. Bake on the top shelf for 8-10 mins until the croutons are golden and tomatoes softened. Set aside.
3. Heat a little oil in a large frying pan on medium-high heat. Add the onion with salt, pepper and an optional pinch of sugar. Fry for 8-10 mins until soft and sweet, then add garlic and cook for 1 min.
4. Add water and vegetable stock paste to the pan. Bring to the boil, then simmer 2-3 mins until reduced by about half. Stir in lentils and harissa paste, bring back to the boil and cook 2-3 mins. Add spinach in handfuls and cook 1-2 mins until wilted. Cover and remove from heat.
5. Drain the halloumi and pat dry with kitchen paper. Heat a little oil in another frying pan on medium-high heat and fry halloumi for 2-3 mins per side until golden.
6. Stir the roasted tomatoes into the lentils and season to taste. Serve lentils in bowls, then top with croutons and halloumi.

## Suggestions

- Add extra vegetables such as peppers or courgettes.
- Reduce added salt if preferred, as halloumi can be quite salty.
- This recipe keeps well; leftovers are good cold for lunch the next day.
- If you want larger portions, increase the lentils.
