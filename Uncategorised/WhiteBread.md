## Instructions

1. Add warm water to the stand mixer bowl.
2. Add salt and oil, then add the flour.
3. Sprinkle yeast on top and lightly mix into the flour with fingertips.
4. Knead with dough hook on low until combined, then on speed 1 for 10 minutes.
5. Shape into a ball, return to bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and leave in a warm draft-free place until doubled (about 1 hour).
6. Lightly oil and flour a 2lb loaf tin.
7. Tip dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead briefly to knock back.
8. Flatten to a rectangle, fold and roll into a tight loaf shape, seam side down.
9. Place in the tin, cover, and prove again until doubled (about 1 to 1.5 hours).
10. Heat oven to 220C fan / 240C / 475F / gas 9.
11. Bake just below the middle shelf for 30 minutes.
12. If crust browns too quickly, reduce heat slightly. Remove from tin and cool on a wire rack.

## Suggestions

- Use digital scales for consistency; several readers reported better results after switching from volume measures.
- Adjust proving time to the dough, not just the clock. Slight overproving can cause collapse or flat tops.
- The same dough can be shaped into rolls (about 10 rolls from one batch) and baked for a shorter time at the same temperature.
- If using mixed or wholemeal flour, increase water slightly and expect shorter first prove times as needed.
- Optional variation: add 75g to 150g grated hard cheese after first rise, then shape and prove as normal.
