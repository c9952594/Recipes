## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 220°C / 200°C fan / gas mark 7. Cut the potatoes into 1cm chips, toss with 15ml olive oil, salt and pepper, then spread on a baking tray. Roast on the top shelf for 25-30 mins, turning halfway.
2. Meanwhile, halve the peppers lengthways and remove the core and seeds. Place cut-side up on a baking tray, drizzle with a little of the oil and season with salt and pepper. Wrap the garlic cloves in foil with a little oil. Roast the peppers and garlic parcel on the middle shelf for 10-12 mins, until the peppers begin to soften.
3. Drain and rinse the lentils. Heat a little oil in a large frying pan over medium-high heat, add the tomato puree and peri peri seasoning, and cook for 1 min. Stir in the lentils, vegetable stock paste, sugar and water. Bring to the boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 3-4 mins until thickened.
4. Stir half the cheese and the optional butter through the lentil mixture. Season to taste. Remove the peppers and garlic from the oven. Fill each pepper half with the lentil ragu, top with the remaining cheese, then bake for 6-8 mins until the cheese is golden and the peppers are soft.
5. Roughly chop the coriander. Zest and halve the lime. Squeeze the roasted garlic from its skins into a small bowl and mash with a fork. Mix with the coriander, lime zest, salt, pepper, an optional pinch of sugar, half the lime juice and 15ml olive oil to make the salsa.
6. In a medium bowl, mix the remaining lime juice with 15ml olive oil, salt and pepper. Add the baby leaves and toss to coat.
7. Serve the stuffed peppers with the chips and salad, then spoon over the coriander salsa.

## Suggestions

- Add feta for extra flavour.
- If you prefer milder heat, reduce the peri peri seasoning.
- This keeps well and makes a good lunch the next day.
- If the peppers are small, use larger ones if available for easier stuffing.
